EVAL QUERIES — semantic search quality check
================================================

HOW TO USE THIS
------------------
Once real embeddings + self-hosted model files are in place (see
MODEL-README.txt), type each query below into "Search Everything" and check
whether the "Expected top hit" appears in the results — ideally in the top
2-3, and ideally tagged "By meaning" if the keyword engine wouldn't have
found it on its own.

This is the actual test of whether this feature is worth shipping. Don't
skip it. If a large fraction of these miss, raise/lower SEM_THRESHOLD in
app-shell.html and re-test before relying on this for real callouts.

Each query below is deliberately phrased the way an officer might actually
say or type it — NOT using the entry's own statute name or exact wording —
specifically to stress-test whether meaning-based matching beats the old
keyword/synonym engine. A query that only works because it happens to share
a literal word with the target entry doesn't prove much; these are chosen to
avoid that.

QUERIES
----------

1.  "guy won't tell me his name on a stop"
    Expected: Failure to Identify (or nearest equivalent arrest/statute entry)

2.  "found a baggie of white powder in his pocket, looked like cocaine"
    Expected: Possession of Controlled Substance, Penalty Group 1 (§ 481.115)

3.  "car search after towing it because it was stolen"
    Expected: Automobile Exception (Carroll Doctrine) or inventory-search related entry

4.  "guy ran when he saw me, high crime neighborhood, nothing else"
    Expected: Illinois v. Wardlow

5.  "dog sniffed the car during a traffic stop, took longer than expected"
    Expected: Rodriguez v. United States

6.  "shot at a fleeing suspect, unarmed, jumping a fence"
    Expected: Tennessee v. Garner

7.  "how much force is reasonable when someone resists"
    Expected: Graham v. Connor

8.  "wife called and said husband hit her, no marks visible"
    Expected: Assault (family violence) and/or mandatory arrest for family violence entry

9.  "vape pen with weird crystals, might be fake weed"
    Expected: Penalty Group 2-A (synthetic cannabinoids / K2, Spice)

10. "pulled over for a busted tail light, turned out the law didn't require it"
    Expected: Heien v. North Carolina

11. "consented to search but his girlfriend who also lives there said no"
    Expected: Georgia v. Randolph

12. "warrant came back active during a routine stop, found drugs after"
    Expected: Utah v. Strieff

13. "someone posting nude pics of an ex without consent"
    Expected: closest revenge-porn / unlawful disclosure statute if present, else Electronic Transmission of Visual Material Depicting Minor (if adult-specific entry doesn't exist, this should surface the closest related entry, not a false-positive minor-specific charge)

14. "guy camping under the overpass with a tent"
    Expected: Prohibited Camping (§ 48.05)

15. "found a fake temporary paper tag on a car that didn't match"
    Expected: Wrong, Fictitious, Altered, or Obscured License Plate (§ 504.945)

16. "kid was texting and swerving near the elementary school"
    Expected: Wireless Communication Device in a School Crossing Zone (§ 545.425)

17. "beer can rolling around under the passenger seat, opened"
    Expected: Possession of Alcoholic Beverage in Motor Vehicle / Open Container (§ 49.031)

18. "guy pointed a gun at someone during an argument, didn't fire"
    Expected: Deadly Conduct (§ 22.05)

19. "search of a phone after arrest without a warrant"
    Expected: Riley v. California

20. "cell tower records to place a suspect somewhere without a warrant"
    Expected: Carpenter v. United States

21. "person having a mental health crisis, threatening to hurt themselves"
    Expected: Emergency Detention by Peace Officer (§ 573.001)

22. "entered a house without a warrant because we heard a kid screaming inside"
    Expected: Brigham City v. Stuart or Case v. Montana (emergency aid doctrine)

23. "chased a guy into his garage for a misdemeanor"
    Expected: Lange v. California

24. "knocked on a door, smelled weed, worried they'd flush it"
    Expected: Kentucky v. King

25. "searched the car after arresting the driver, he was already cuffed in the back seat"
    Expected: Arizona v. Gant

26. "does silence count as invoking your rights during an interrogation"
    Expected: Miranda v. Arizona

27. "someone selling counterfeit prescription pills that aren't controlled substances"
    Expected: Delivery or Offer of Delivery of Dangerous Drug (§ 483.042) or Forging/Altering a Prescription (§ 483.045)

28. "third DUI-adjacent stop, driver still driving on a suspended license"
    Expected: Driving While License Invalid (§ 521.457)

29. "guy huffing computer duster to get high"
    Expected: Possession and Use of Abusable Volatile Chemical (§ 485.031)

30. "motorcycle rider with no helmet, said he's over 21"
    Expected: Offenses Relating to Not Wearing Protective Headgear (§ 661.003)

31. "someone swatted a house, called in a fake hostage situation"
    Expected: closest false-report/swatting-related entry

32. "officer wants free online training that doesn't need his department's login"
    Expected: one or more Training tab entries (Classen-Buck, OSS Academy, Virtual Academy, or TRN)

33. "civilian filmed us during an arrest, can they do that"
    Expected: closest doctrine/entry addressing recording of officers, if one exists — otherwise confirm no false-positive dominates

34. "does the smell of marijuana alone justify a full vehicle search now"
    Expected: Automobile Exception (Carroll Doctrine), possibly cross-referenced with current hemp-vs-marihuana distinction note in § 481.121

35. "found a stolen firearm in a car during a traffic stop, driver on probation"
    Expected: could span several entries (UUV, Unlawful Carrying Weapons, Automobile Exception) — check that the results are sensibly split, not just one dominant false match

NOTES FOR CALIBRATION
------------------------
Queries 13, 22, 31, and 33 are intentionally "does an entry like this even
exist yet" tests — a legitimate result there might be "no great match," and
that's fine information, not a failure. What matters is that the app doesn't
confidently hand back something unrelated in those cases.

If several queries above return zero "By meaning" tags at all even though
plain-keyword search also comes up empty, that likely means SEM_ENABLED is
false (embeddings/model not wired up yet) rather than a quality problem —
check that before concluding the threshold needs adjusting.
