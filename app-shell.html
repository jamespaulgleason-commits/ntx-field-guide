Arrest Authority
Jurisdiction
Texas — CCP Chapter 14. Warrantless arrest authority is state-specific.
Keyword search
Your scenario
Two locks on every warrantless arrest in Texas. An arrest must be authorized by a specific statutory exception (CCP Chapter 14 or another statute) and supported by Fourth Amendment probable cause. Texas law is more restrictive than the federal floor — and under Art. 38.23, evidence from an arrest that violates the statute can be suppressed even when the Constitution was satisfied.

Keyword search
Ordinance category
Local ordinances supplement, not replace, state law. A city ordinance cannot authorize conduct state law forbids, and most Class C ordinance violations are fine-only with no custodial arrest unless another statutory hook applies (e.g., failure to identify, outstanding warrant). Always confirm an ordinance is currently in force — cities amend their codes routinely — before relying on it.
